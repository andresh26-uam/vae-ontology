Substitutabilityc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/def/vaeontology_moral_values_in_norm_DM#Substitutability

"The substitutability relation is a binary relation Rs ⊆ N × N. If (ni , nj) ∈ Rs , we say that norms ni , nj are interchangeable or substitutable. Based on substitutability relationships, we introduce the notion ofsubstitution chain as follows. Given two norms, ni ,nk ∈ N, we say that nk is connected by substitutabilities to ni ifthere is a non-empty subset of norms {n1, . . . , np} ⊆ N such that (n1, n2), ..., (np−1, np)∈ Rs , n1 = ni , and np = nk. Henceforth, a new relationship S ⊆ N ×N will contain the pairs of norms that are connected by substitutabilities. In particular, notice that if (ni , nj) ∈ Rs , then (ni , nj) ∈ S. Rs is an irreflexive, symmetric, and transitive relation."
has super-classes
irreflexive relation statement, symmetric relation statement, transitive relation statement

Transitive Generalization Statementc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/def/vaeontology_moral_values_in_norm_DM#TransitiveGeneralizationStatement

IMPORTANT NOTE: To keep consistency, when creating a graph of generalizations you must normally link the nodes using DirectGeneralizationStatements. When you expect transitivity on any element of the graph (i.e. that it generalizes all of its offspring) you must create a new TransitiveGeneralizationStatement object and a hasFirstElement relation with that object to the said element. Automatically, every consecutive Direct generalization that goes after a node that is the secondElement of another TransitiveGeneralizationStatement will be considered as a secondElement of the created TransitiveGeneralizationStatement (see rules vaemvndmP1-2). This caveat is necessary as the two classes of statements are disjoint (DirectGeneralizationStatement is anti-transitive and TransitiveGeneralizationStatement is transitive) and with SWRL rules it is not possible to create new individuals based on others.

In this example, MinimumAgeForDriving isDefinedIn the Norm ItalianTrafficCodeAgeDriving. More complex parametrization requires workarounds. E.g. AgeRangeForDrugUsage could parametrize data value: 14 to 50 on the datatype: xsd:int. Since complex datatypes are not allowed in OWL1.0, a solution to this can only work by creating two 'sub-parameters': MinimumAgeForDrugUsage (that hasParameterDataValue 14) and MaximumAgeForDrugUsage (that hasParameterDataValue 50), which are components of (cf. hasComponent) the main Parameter AgeRangeForDrugUsage. Ordering on subparameters can be created by using or specializing the object property 'precedes'.
